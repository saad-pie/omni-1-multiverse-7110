What is a Qubit?

Unlike classical bits that are either 0 or 1, a quantum bit (qubit) can be 0, 1, or a superposition of both simultaneously. This allows for vastly more complex computations.

Superposition Explained

Superposition is the ability of a quantum system to exist in multiple states at once. Imagine a spinning coin that is both heads and tails until it lands.

Quantum Entanglement

Entanglement is a phenomenon where two or more qubits become linked, sharing the same fate even when separated by vast distances. Measuring one instantly affects the other.
